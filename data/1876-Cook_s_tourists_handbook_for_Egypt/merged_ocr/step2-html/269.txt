The spotted hyena, the ibex, the gazelle, the coney, and a<br/>
few other animals, dwell amongst these mountains, but they<br/>
are very <span style="color: #0000FF;">difficult </span>of approach. The birds also are exceedingly shy; a few species of partridge, and the sand<br/>
grouse, and the quail, are met with. Near the Red Sea teal<br/>
and duck and other water-fowl are occasionally found. The<br/>
climate is intensely dry, and very healthy. The old hermits<br/>
asserted that the desert air was so life-giving, that man<br/>
scarcely needed either sustenance or repose.<br/>
