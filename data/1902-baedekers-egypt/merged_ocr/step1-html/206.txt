ARRIVAL AT ALEXANDRIA (comp. Map, p. <span style="color: #ff0000;">18 </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">]</span>. The perfectly<br/>
flat <span style="color: #0000FF;">NE</span>. coast of Egypt, and even Alexandria itself, are not visible<br/>
to the steamboat-passenger until very shortly before the vessel enters<br/>
the harbour. We first observe the lighthouse. The steamer takes a<br/>
pilot on board and is steered by him through a narrow, shallow, and<br/>
rocky channel (<span style="color: #ff0000;">Béghaz</span>) into the harbour. As the passage can only<br/>
be effected by daylight, vessels arriving in the evening must ride at<br/>
anchor outside until next morning. To the left, on the <span style="color: #ff0000;">Rh </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">et-TM<br/>
</span>('Cape of Figs'), are the <span style="color: #ff0000;">vieeroy</span>'s palace and the arsenal. Most of<br/>
the steamers lie alongside the quays.<br/>

As soon as the brief sanitary inspection is over, the porters and<br/>
<span style="color: #ff0000;">commissionnaires </span>swarm wildly on deck, <span style="color: #0000FF;">vociferating </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">in </span>half-a-dozen<br/>
different languages, and with animated <span style="color: #ff0000;">gestieulations </span>precipitate<br/>
themselves <span style="color: #0000FF;">upon </span>the travellers' luggage. The best plan, especially when ladies are of the party, is to secure the services of one of<br/>
Cook<span style="color: #0000FF;">'s </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">or </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">Game</span>'s agents, who relieves the traveller of all trouble,<br/>
assists in clearing luggage at the <span style="color: #ff0000;">eustom-house</span>, and conducts the<br/>
travellers to the special omnibuses for the hotels or <span style="color: #0000FF;">railway-station</span>.<br/>
These agents are recognizable by the <span style="color: #ff0000;">oftieial </span>caps, while the Arabs<br/>
