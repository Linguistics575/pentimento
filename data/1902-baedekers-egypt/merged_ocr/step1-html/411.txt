Port <span style="color: #0000FF;">Sa'id</span>, the chief town of the Egyptian province of the<br/>
Isthmus and Suez Canal, lies at the E. extremity of an island Which<br/>
belongs to the narrow strip of land separating Lake Menzaleh from<br/>
the Mediterranean. It owes its origin to the Suez Canal, and its<br/>
prosperity during the last ten years has been so marked that it has<br/>
become a serious rival to Alexandria. The population, Which in 1883<br/>
was only 17,000, is now about 42,000, including 11,300 Europeans.<br/>
