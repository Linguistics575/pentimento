CAIRO TO ISMAILIA<br/>
<br/>
FOR two hours one traverses the Delta, swiftly and smoothly,<br/>
through rich cotton and <span style="color: #0000FF;">corn </span>land, bridging canals that flow<br/>
peacefully between long rows of <span style="color: #0000FF;">mimosa-trees</span>. To the right<br/>
is the pale rose border of low hills that stretch from Marg<br/>
past Belbeis, along the desert line to <span style="color: #0000FF;">Abou-Hammad</span>. There<br/>
the cultivation ceases. From Abou-Hammad to <span style="color: #0000FF;">Tel-el-Kebir<br/>
</span>is <span style="color: #0000FF;">sand- </span>uncompromising sand, and only sand.<br/>
<br/>
The brief halt at <span style="color: #0000FF;">Tel-el-Kebir </span>shows the cemetery on the<br/>
right hand-a green patch, quiet with memories. Again<br/>
there is sand and waste until Ismailia is reached. There is<br/>
complete <span style="color: #0000FF;">change-a </span>leafy hamlet in the midst of desert.<br/>
Everywhere is thick foliage of lebbek-trees above carpets of<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">flowers </span>and vegetation, intersected by straight white <span style="color: #0000FF;">roads</span>.<br/>
