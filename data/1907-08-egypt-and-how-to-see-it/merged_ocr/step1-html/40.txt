THE ARAB MUSEUM<br/>
<br/>
This is in the same building as the Khedivial Library, but<br/>
the entrance is round the corner to the left from the latter<span style="color: #ff0000;">,<br/>
</span>in the Midan <span style="color: #0000FF;">Bab-el-Khalk</span>.<br/>
<br/>
It is open in winter every day except Fridays and Mohammedan holidays from <span style="color: #0000FF;">9 </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">AM</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">. </span>till 4.30 RM.<br/>
<br/>
A catalogue is on sale at the price of 20 pt.<br/>
<br/>
The very fine collection of Arab antiquities, brought in<br/>
many cases from old Cairo mosques, but also from Upper<br/>
and Lower Egypt, is divided amongst fifteen rooms. The<br/>
different objects are arranged separately, and as far as<br/>
possible in <span style="color: #0000FF;">chronological </span>order.<br/>
<br/>
Room 1 contains objects in stone and marble, <span style="color: #0000FF;">chiefly<br/>
</span>funerary stones.<br/>
<br/>
Room 2 contains ornamental objects in stone and marble.<br/>
<br/>
Room 3 contains mosaics<span style="color: #ff0000;">, </span>casts, & c., and some very fine<br/>
examples <span style="color: #ff0000;">of </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">mushrabieh </span>work, a large chandelier from the<br/>
Sultan <span style="color: #0000FF;">Human </span>Mosque, and other interesting objects in<br/>
marble.<br/>
<br/>
Room 4 contains some very old carvings in wood, leaves of<br/>
a door from the <span style="color: #0000FF;">mosque </span>of Kalaoun, prayer niches and <span style="color: #0000FF;">reading<br/>
</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">stands </span>for the Koran, <span style="color: #0000FF;">Ire</span>.<br/>
<br/>
Room 5 contains four massive old doors, two dating from<br/>
the thirteenth century and two from Turkish times, <span style="color: #ff0000;">mushrabieh </span>work, lattices, <span style="color: #0000FF;">& </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">c.<br/>
<br/>
</span>Room 6 contains <span style="color: #ff0000;">carved-wood </span>ornaments and doors.<br/>
<br/>
Room 7 contains Arabic tables beautifully inlaid with<br/>
ivory, wooden nimbar stairs and pulpits from mosques, boxes<br/>
to hold the Koran, <span style="color: #0000FF;">& </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">c.<br/>
<br/>
</span>Room 8 contains <span style="color: #ff0000;">mushrabieh </span>work, pulpits, and mosaic<br/>

<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">Room </span>9 and 10 contain metal work, bronze plated doors,<br/>
