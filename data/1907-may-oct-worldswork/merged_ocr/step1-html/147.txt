</span>It is a cardinal principle of forestry that only<br/>
land not permanently suited to agriculture<br/>
should be used for growing timber<span style="color: #0000FF;">, </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">From </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">onefifth </span>to one-third of the land area is about the<br/>

South, with over one-half of its area wooded,<br/>
can stand a good deal of clearing up. As scientific agriculture gives greater productive power<br/>
to farming land, as cities grow in size and<br/>
increase the demand for food, and as the rural<br/>
population multiplies so that more land can be<br/>
manned, it will pay better and <span style="color: #0000FF;">better </span>to bring<br/>

growing knowledge of how to make American<br/>
woodland most productive, justify expectations<br/>
of an increase in the bid which forestry will put<br/>
in for use of the land.<br/>
