Though the treasures of the Ghizeh Museum and the
Pyramids astonish us; it is only by visiting the monu-
ments of Upper Egypt that we can get a concise idea of
the high degree of civilisation to which the Ancient
Egyptians had attained.

The voyage to Upper Egypt, which was formerly so
difficult and expensive, has become, by means of the
commodious steamers now on the Nile, a very pleasant
pleasure trip, available even to persons of moderate
means.

The steamboats, dahabiyehs, and Hotels at Louxor
and Assouan have been expressly built with all the com-
fort and luxury desirable, and travellers will not fail to
find all that consideration to which they have been
accustomed.

The firm of Cook & Son also presents its clients, who
visit Upper Egypt, with a beautiful Guide, ably written
by E. A. WALLIS BUDGE, F.S.A., Acting-Assistant
Keeper in the Department of Egyptian and Assyrian
Antiquities, British Museum.
