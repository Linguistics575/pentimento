<span>The Second Pyramid.<br/>
<br/>
This pyramid seems at first sight to be greater than<br/>
the first, but that is simply because it is constructed on<br/>
a higher level.<br/>
<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">It </span>was erected by <span style="color: #0000FF;">Chti</span>--<span style="color: #0000FF;">-iiii </span>or Chefren, the third<br/>
king of the IVth dynasty<span style="color: #ff0000;">: </span>his name, however, has not<br/>
yet been discovered on any part of the monument. There<br/>
are two entrances on the north side, one at a height of<br/>
<span style="color: #ff0000;">50-ft.</span><span style="color: #ff0000;">, </span>which leads into two mortuary chambers, the<br/>
uppermost of which contains the granite sarcophagus of<br/>
Chefren. The present height of this pyramid is about<br/>
450-ft., base <span style="color: #0000FF;">700-it</span>. It is not at all advisable to attempt<br/>
the ascent of it, although the Bedouins ascend with comparative ease.<br/>
<br/>
It was searched by <span style="color: #0000FF;">Belzoni </span>in the year 1816, Near<br/>
this pyramid are some remains of a temple intended for<br/>
the worship of the dead.<br/>
