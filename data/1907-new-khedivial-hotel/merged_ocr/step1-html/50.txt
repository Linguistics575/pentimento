</span>The <span style="color: #0000FF;">Mokattam </span>Mountain.<br/>
<br/>
The Mokattam Mountain or <span style="color: #0000FF;">Gebel </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">Gitishi</span>, so called<br/>
after the ruined mosque on its summit, lies east of the<br/>
town.<br/>
<br/>
It is formed of limestone containing numberless masses<br/>
of petrified nummulites from the size of a lentil seed to<br/>
that of a shilling, as well as many other very interesting<br/>
petrifications<span style="color: #ff0000;">, </span>such as <span style="color: #0000FF;">sea-urchins</span>, oysters<span style="color: #ff0000;">, </span>nautili<span style="color: #ff0000;">.<br/>
</span>sharks' teeth, etc., and sundry crystals.<br/>
<br/>
The rock furnishes building material that has been<br/>
much prized during many ages. The Ghizeh pyramids<br/>
were erected of blocks from this mountain, whose sides<br/>
are pierced by a number of very interesting quarries and<br/>
caves) which are well worth a visit.<br/>
<br/>
In visiting the Mokattam it is better to use donkeys,<br/>
especially if there are ladies going, as the excursion on<br/>
foot is too tiring.<br/>
