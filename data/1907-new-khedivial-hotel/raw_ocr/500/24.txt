On the east side of the town, at the foot of the Mokat-
tam Mountain, are the tomb mosques of the Circassian
Mameluke Sultans who reigned over Egypt from A.D.
1381-1517, known by the name of the Tombs of the
Khalifs.

These fine buildings with their bold and elegant domes
and slender minarets may be numbered among the most

beautiful examples of Arabian art, and deserve to be
frequently visited when time permits.

To reach the Tombs of the Khalifs leave the town by
the gate of Biib-en-Nasr, cross the Mohammedan
cemetery, where L. J. Burkhardt, the famous Swiss
traveller is buried (1817), and follow a dusty road, leaving
the hill of the windmills to the right.
