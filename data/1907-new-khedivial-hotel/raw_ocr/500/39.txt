The Sphinx and the Pyramids.

The Great Pyramid.

This, the greatest of the Ghizeh pyramids, was erected
by Chufu (the Cheops of the Greek), the second king of
the IVth dynasty 3.0. 3733.

The present measurement of each side is 755-ft. at the
base and 451-ft. high, but the original dimensions were
greater by 20 or 30-ft. The blocks of stone, of which
it is constructed, are from the quarries of Mokattam and
Turra, and the contents amount to 85,000,000 cubic
feet.

The ascension is not dimeult, but very tiring, because
of the height of the stone blocks which average 3-ft.
high.

Starting at the north east angle, aided by two or three
Bedouins, the top is reached in about 15 minutes. A
