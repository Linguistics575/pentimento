It has several Public Rooms- Drawing, Sitting,
Smoking, in various styles, decorated with palms and
shrubs, and a splendid Dining Room in Arabesque style.

The Cuisine is in the hands of first-class cooks, and as
only best provisions are supplied it will not fail to satisfy
the most delicate tastes.

A Telephone, near the Hall Porter, is at the disposal of
the guests, and dragomen conversant with all European
languages are attached to the premises.

Saloon.

An official Letter-Box is at the entrance of the Hotel,
which is regularly cleared by the Post Office for the
convenience of the guests.

The Hotel is open all the year round.

An omnibus and interpreter attend the arrival and
departure of the trains, and interpreters are stationed at
both the ports of Alexandria and Port Said for the con-
venience of the passengers.

The Hotel provides accommodation for over a hundred
people.
