CHAPTER XI

THE LIFE OF THE "FELLAH"

"IN the sweat of thy brow shalt thou eat bread" is
a truth which is brought home to the minds of all who
mix with the people, and no one who has lived among
the "fellahin "could fail to be struck by their patient
industry.

At early dawn man and beast go forth to their
labour, and only at nightfall do they cease their indefatigable toil. Thrifty to a degree, not an inch of
land is wasted, and it is a matter of common observation that neither grass meadow nor wild-flower may be
found in cultivated Egypt, for land which can produce
these will support a better crop. Field-work is carried
on with the most primitive of implements, but the
land is well tilled, and planted with such a rotation of
crops as experience has proved most profitable.
