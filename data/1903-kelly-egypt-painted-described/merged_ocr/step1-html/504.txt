<span>CHAPTER XIII<br/>
RELIGION AND CHARACTER OF THE EGYPTIANS<br/>
<br/>
IT is very difficult for a European to form any just<br/>
estimate of the character of an Oriental, who is usually<br/>

is it easy for the Western mind to fully appreciate<br/>
the ideas or motives which dominate the action of the<br/>
Moslem.<br/>

</span>and hybrid population of Cairo, we will again take the<br/>
