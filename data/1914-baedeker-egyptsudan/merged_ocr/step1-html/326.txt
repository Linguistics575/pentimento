<span style="color: #ff0000;">3</span>. The <span style="color: #ff0000;">Mohattam </span>Hills.<br/>
<br/>
An excursion to the <span style="color: #ff0000;">Mohattam </span>Hills is best made from the citadel (tram<br/>
ways Nos. 2, 6, and 13, to the Place Saladin, see p. 38). The route from<br/>
the Tombs of the Caliphs is to be avoided on account of the intolerable<br/>
dust. The excursion may be combined with the visit to the smaller Petrified<br/>
Forest in the manner indicated at <span style="color: #0000FF;">p. </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">IN</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">. </span>The <span style="color: #0000FF;">View </span>is one of the most<br/>
beautiful that Egypt has to <span style="color: #0000FF;">offer</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">, </span>and no energetic traveller should be<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">satisfied </span>with the <span style="color: #0000FF;">substitutes </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">afforded </span>by the citadel (near the mosque <span style="color: #0000FF;">of<br/>
</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">Mohammed </span>Ali, see p. 69) <span style="color: #ff0000;">or </span>the Windmill Hills (p. <span style="color: #ff0000;">ilk</span>)<span style="color: #ff0000;">. </span>It is best at<br/>
sunset <span style="color: #0000FF;">or </span>in the morning between 8 <span style="color: #ff0000;">and </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">9 </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">o'clock</span><span style="color: #ff0000;">. </span>A visit at the time of<br/>
the full <span style="color: #0000FF;">moon </span>is attractive also.<br/>
<br/>
From the Citadel (<span style="color: #ff0000;">p. </span>68) the <span style="color: #0000FF;">route </span>ascends in an almost straight<br/>
direction, passing through the <span style="color: #0000FF;">Bab </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">el-Gebel </span>(P1. F, <span style="color: #0000FF;">6</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">; </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">p. </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">70</span>) and<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">over </span>the railway-bridge. <span style="color: #0000FF;">About </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">1/2 </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">hr</span>. brings us to the top.<br/>
<br/>
The <span style="color: #ff0000;">Mohattam </span>or <span style="color: #0000FF;">Moqattam </span>Hills (666 ft. high), to the E. of<br/>
Cairo, also called Gebel <span style="color: #ff0000;">Gigashi</span>, after the <span style="color: #0000FF;">conspicuous </span>mosque situated on the summit, belong to the great range of <span style="color: #ff0000;">nummulitie </span>limestone mountains which extend from <span style="color: #0000FF;">N.W</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">. </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">Africa</span>, across Egypt and<br/>
India, to China. This nummulite formation is one of the <span style="color: #ff0000;">eoeene</span>, or<br/>
oldest deposits of the tertiary period. It yields a favourite <span style="color: #ff0000;">buildingstone</span><span style="color: #ff0000;">, </span>and there are numerous quarries on the slopes of the hills.<br/>
<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">Nummulitic </span>limestone is remarkably rich in fossils, the <span style="color: #ff0000;">chiefmass </span>of<br/>
which consists of millions of <span style="color: #0000FF;">nummulites </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">or </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">rhizopods </span>of the <span style="color: #0000FF;">polythalamia </span>group<span style="color: #ff0000;">. </span>The larger kinds are about an inch and a half in diameter<br/>
and the smaller about <span style="color: #0000FF;">1/5 </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">inch</span>. They are frequently seen also in the<br/>
stones of the Pyramids, part of the material for which was taken from<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">the </span>quarries of the <span style="color: #ff0000;">Mohattam</span>. The quarries yield <span style="color: #0000FF;">also </span>a profusion <span style="color: #ff0000;">orseaurchins </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">Celypetrster</span>, <span style="color: #0000FF;">cidaris</span>, <span style="color: #ff0000;">echinolampas</span>, <span style="color: #ff0000;">ete</span>.), various kinds of bivalves<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">(</span>including many oysters), cerithium, ovula, <span style="color: #0000FF;">strombus</span>, <span style="color: #0000FF;">nerite</span>, turritella<span style="color: #0000FF;">,<br/>
</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">nautilus</span>, sharks, teeth, and bones of <span style="color: #ff0000;">the </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">halicore</span>. Beautiful crystals of<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">isinglass-stone </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">and </span>of <span style="color: #0000FF;">strontian </span>also occur.<br/>
<br/>
The <span style="color: #0000FF;">"</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">VIEW </span>from the top is <span style="color: #0000FF;">magnificent </span>and in a good light is<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">finer </span>than any other in the neighbourhood of Cairo. The citadel,<br/>
the mosque of <span style="color: #ff0000;">Mohammed </span>Ali, and the grand burial-grounds of the<br/>
desert form a noble foreground; the venerable Nile dotted with its<br/>
lateen sails <span style="color: #0000FF;">flows </span>below us in its quiet majesty; to the W., on the<br/>
borders of the immeasurable desert, tower the huge and wondrous<br/>
old Pyramids, gilded and reddened by the setting sun. The thousand<br/>
minarets of the city and the citadel are then also tinted with a<br/>
delicate rosy hue. A still more varied view is commanded by a<br/>
steep projection to the S. of the mosque, the foreground being<br/>
especially <span style="color: #0000FF;">picturesque</span>, while the horizon to the S. seems more open<br/>
and tempts our fancy to visit the wonders of Upper Egypt.<br/>
