EGYPT proper, the country between the mouth of the Nile and<br/>
the First Cataract, is a small region with <span style="color: #0000FF;">well-defined </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">natural </span>boundaries on three sides. <span style="color: #0000FF;">On </span>the N. is the Mediterranean Sea, on the <span style="color: #ff0000;">E.<br/>
</span>the <span style="color: #0000FF;">Arabian </span>Desert and the Red Sea, and on the W. the Libyan <span style="color: #0000FF;">Desert</span>. The S. boundary is not marked by any natural feature, and has<br/>

N. and S. form a <span style="color: #0000FF;">kind </span>of standard <span style="color: #0000FF;">of </span>the political power of Egypt,<br/>
and the causes of the variations involve a great part of Egyptian<br/>
history from the most ancient times down to the present day.<br/>
