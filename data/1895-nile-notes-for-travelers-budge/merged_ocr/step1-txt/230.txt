206 NOTES FOR TRAVELLERS IN EGYPT.

Room LXXVH.-Priests of Amen.

1137, 1138. Coffins of children.

1140. Coffin of Ankhesmesit, a lady in the college of AmenRa at Thebes.

1141. Coffin of Tanneferef, a "divine father" and priest of
the highest rank.

1145. Coffin of'l'irpu, a lady in the college of Amen-Ra.

1146. Coffin of Ankh-f-en-Mut, a "divine father," which
originally belonged to a lady whose name still stands
upon it.

1147. Coffin of Ankh-f-en-Mut, a priest of Mut, and scribe
of the estates of Amen, and priest of the Queen Aur.
hetep.

1148 a and b. Covers of coffins of Peg-Amen, a scribe of
the granaries of Amen-Ra.

Room LXXX.-Priests of Amen.
1150. Cover of a coffin of Pa-khare, surnamed Khamefetu
Amen, a "divine father."

1151 a and b. Coffins of Nesesta-pen-hertahat, fourth
prophet of Amen.

1152. Coffin of Pets-Amen, an official of Amen, Nut and
Khonsu.
