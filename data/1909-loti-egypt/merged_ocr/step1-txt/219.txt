Its antiquity was already legendary in the time
of Alexander the Great, on whose behalf a chapel
was added to it; and later on, in the first ages
of Christianity, a corner of the ruins was turned
into a cathedral. The tourists begin to depart,
for the lunch bell calls them to the neighbouring
tables d'Mte; and while I wait till they shall be
gone, I occupy myself in following the bas-reliefs
which are displayed for a length of more than
a hundred yards along the base of the walls. It
is one long row of people moving in their
thousands all in the same direction-the ritual
procession of the God Amen. With the care
which characterised the Egyptians to draw
everything from life so as to render it eternal,
there are represented here the smallest details
of a day of festival three or four thousand years
ago. And how like it is to a holiday of the
people of to-day! Along the route of the procession are ranged jugglers and sellers of drinks
and fruits, and negro acrobats who walk on their
hands and twist themselves into all kinds of
contortions. But the procession itself was evidently of a magnificence such as we no longer
