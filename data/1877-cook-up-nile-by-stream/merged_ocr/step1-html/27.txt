</span><span style="color: #0000FF;">:26 </span>UP THE NILE BY STEAM.<br/>
<br/>
The same day brings us to Dakkeh (10 miles), where there is a<br/>
temple supposed to have been built by the Ethiopian King <span style="color: #ff0000;">Ergamun</span><span style="color: #ff0000;">.<br/>
</span>a part of it is in a very good state of preservation. The sculptures<br/>
and hieroglyphics are extremely good, and in admirable condition.<br/>
<br/>
The steamer proceeds for some time yet and possibly reaches<br/>
Maharraka, seven miles from Dakkeh; here is a small temple, of<br/>
which but two rows of columns remain entire.<br/>
<br/>
FOURTH DAY.<br/>
<br/>
(14 miles.) Arrive <span style="color: #0000FF;">at </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">Saboa </span>or <span style="color: #ff0000;">Wady-Saboah</span>, "The Valley<br/>
of the <span style="color: #0000FF;">Lions</span>." It has little to indicate its former splendour.<br/>
The temple of Rameses II. is nearly buried in the sand. Two<br/>
statues, however, remain in sight, <span style="color: #0000FF;">which </span>may induce a short<br/>
visit.<br/>
<br/>
Arrive at Korosko, 16 miles <span style="color: #0000FF;">further</span>, and about 100 miles from<br/>
<span style="color: #ff0000;">Philae</span>. It is from here that the great high road stretches across<br/>
the Desert to Shendy and Sennaar. Most of the caravans for<br/>
<span style="color: #ff0000;">Khartoom </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">start </span>from Korosko. Behind the village is a peak<br/>
called "<span style="color: #0000FF;">Awes-el-Guarany</span>," the top of which is held sacred by<br/>
the natives, and is a point of pilgrimage, as being the tomb of the<br/>
saint from whom the mountain derives its name. The Steamer<br/>
remains at Korosko five hours, to enable the passengers to<br/>
accomplish the ascent of this peak-a rather <span style="color: #0000FF;">difficult </span>task, over a<br/>
steep path <span style="color: #0000FF;">full </span>of volcanic remains. The journey will repay the<br/>
fatigue, the view from that spot being splendid, commanding<br/>
on one side the Nile <span style="color: #0000FF;">Valley</span>, and on the other the desert road<br/>
winding its course amid rocky hills, and close to the village and<br/>
the road itself numberless caravans lying at rest.<br/>
<br/>

<br/>
Stop at A'mada, about 8 miles from Korosko, and there visit<br/>
a very interesting little temple.<br/>
