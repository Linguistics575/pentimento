S E <span style="color: #0000FF;">C </span>T I <span style="color: #0000FF;">o </span>N I I.<br/>
FROM THE FIRST TO THE <span style="color: #0000FF;">SECOND </span>CATARACT.<br/>
<br/>
The Khedivie Administration having placed a Steamer on the<br/>
Upper Nile, to ply between <span style="color: #0000FF;">Philae </span>and Wadi-Halfa, we inaugurated this new service last season. The experiment having<br/>
proved <span style="color: #ff0000;">a. </span>success, the Administration agreed to <span style="color: #0000FF;">supply </span>a<br/>
larger and more comfortable steamer than the one with which we<br/>
worked that service last year. The voyage to <span style="color: #0000FF;">Wadi-Halfa </span>and<br/>
back may be accomplished in 12 days, of which four and a half<br/>
are employed in sight-seeing ashore, and, after the return journey,<br/>
passengers will have to make the Upper N ile Steamer their hotel<br/>
for two or three extra days, waiting the arrival of the Lower<br/>
Nile Steamer at Assouan to take them back to Cairo. These<br/>
few days will be found anything but unpleasant by those who<br/>
delight in <span style="color: #0000FF;">a </span>soft climate and admire the beauties of nature.<br/>
<span style="color: #0000FF;">Varied </span>scenes may be enjoyed every day, either by going<br/>
in a boat around the Island, or down the river <span style="color: #ff0000;">throngh<br/>
</span>the <span style="color: #0000FF;">numberless </span>rapids which precede the Cataracts, or by riding<br/>
to the neighbouring villages and the granite hills which border<br/>
the different camel paths towards <span style="color: #0000FF;">Assouan</span>. Were it only as a<br/>
resting-place, <span style="color: #0000FF;">Philae </span>would still be found delightful. Although<br/>
navigation on the Upper Nile is quite as easy and secure, if not<br/>
easier than that on the Lower <span style="color: #ff0000;">N </span><span style="color: #ff0000;">ile</span>, it is desirable to <span style="color: #0000FF;">allow </span><span style="color: #0000FF;">three<br/>
</span>days' margin in case of any unforeseen delay above the First<br/>
Cataract.<br/>

by which we continue our voyage'is waiting our arrival; our<br/>
